Suites of Euston
Charlottetown Hospitality At Its Best

     The Suites of Euston neighbourhood was once the site of executions by hanging, even for crimes as benign as stealing loaves of bread. That’s why they called it Gallows Hill, but if you visit us today, you’ll have a decidedly more luxurious experience. We promise. We’re in the business of spoiling our guests, not hanging them. And we give away our bread willingly. You won’t have to steal it unless you really want to.

     When The Suites of Euston was built, the local belles were still wearing bodices, and the electric light bulb didn’t exist. We’ve come a long way since then, so we swear your suite will have all the lightbulbs you’ll ever need—along with all the modern luxuries you expect from a boutique hotel. You’ll have wi-fi, air conditioning, LED television, and a town car service. If you’re one of those strange people who like exercise, we’ll even throw in bike storage. Bring your pooch because we’re pet-friendly, and ... [our local park or dog park] is perfect for a game of fetch.

     Suites of Euston has become one of Charlottetown’s most iconic properties. We’ve kept its heritage in its Victorian fireplaces, hooded dormers, and stunning bay windows. This is the site of Canada’s most charming and underrated neighbourhoods, so when you’re not stealing bread from our guests for some reason, you can catch live music on Victoria Row or grab a piece of chocolatey heaven at the local chocolatier.

     Our charming hotel has seen many guests come and go. If you listen carefully enough, you’ll hear their stories whispering from the walls. Even Stompin’ Tom Connors spent some time here when we were just a boarding house. We are, of course, here to give you your own stories to tell, so get ready for the holiday of the century.

Anonymous. At Suites of Euston. 2025.
